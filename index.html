About Our Madrasa

A Legacy of Islamic Education for Girls

The contemporary interpretation of the teachings of the Holy Prophet and the Ahlul Bayt (peace be upon them) is an essential need of the present era. We live in a time when women, equipped with modern education, are actively contributing to various fields through their individual efforts and practical engagement. Therefore, it is imperative to provide them with a sound religious and intellectual vision. Murabbi-e-Millat Hujjat-ul-Islam wal-Muslimeen Al-Hajj Maulana Syed Haider Mehdi Zaidi (may his blessings continue) recognized the needs of contemporary women and founded a distinguished religious institution to connect them with moral values and authentic Islamic principles. To further this noble mission, he and his esteemed wife, Syeda Rabab Zaidi, established a prominent religious institution on 20 Jumadi al-Thani 1415 AH (corresponding to 24 November 1994) under the name Jamiatuz Zahra in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh.

Jamiatuz Zahra is an academic, Islamic, and madrasa-style seminary institution. It holds the distinction of being the first Shia girls' madrasa in India. Today, the institution is widely recognized for providing comprehensive religious and moral education to girls and, through them, continues to play a significant role in the reform and development of society across various cities in India.

Quranic Studies

Our Quranic Studies program focuses on developing accurate recitation, strong memorization skills, and a deep spiritual connection with the Holy Qur’an. Students are guided in Tajweed, Hifz, and understanding selected verses under qualified female scholars, ensuring both correctness and reverence in learning.

Islamic Sciences

The Islamic Sciences curriculum provides a structured and comprehensive study of core religious subjects including Fiqh, Aqaaid, Hadith, Tafseer, and foundational classical texts. The program aims to equip students with authentic knowledge, critical understanding, and the ability to apply Islamic teachings in daily life.

Character Building

Character Building is at the heart of our mission. Through structured tarbiyat, disciplined routine, and practical guidance, students develop strong Islamic morals, responsibility, humility, and leadership qualities. Education at Jamia-tuz-Zahra shapes not only the intellect but the personality and conduct of every student.

Our Leadership

Founders

Founder

Murabbi-e-Millat Al-Hajj Maulana Syed Haider Mehdi Zaidi

Founder of Jamiatuz zahra

Murabbi-e-Millat Alhajj Maulana Syed Haider Mehdi Zaidi (T.S.) was a visionary scholar, reformer, and devoted servant of Islam whose life was dedicated to the revival of authentic religious education and the moral upliftment of society. At a time when structured and advanced religious education for women was limited and opportunities were scarce, he foresaw the urgent need to establish an organized institution that would provide authentic Islamic scholarship along with disciplined moral training. His vision was not merely to open a madrasa, but to initiate a transformative movement for women’s Islamic education. By the grace of Allah, he formally established Jamia-tuz-Zahra on 20th Jamadi al-Thani 1415 AH (24 November 1994). This historic foundation marked a new chapter in women’s Hawza education, creating a platform where knowledge and tarbiyat would go hand in hand. He profoundly stated: “Educating a man educates an individual, but educating a woman educates an entire society.” This belief became the cornerstone of his mission. He understood that empowered, knowledgeable, and morally grounded women shape generations and strengthen the very fabric of the Ummah. Through relentless effort, sacrifice, and unwavering reliance upon Allah, he laid strong institutional foundations built upon discipline, sincerity, and academic excellence. He firmly believed that education must go beyond textbooks — it must refine character, strengthen faith, and prepare individuals to serve the Ummah with integrity and wisdom. Maulana Zaidi (T.S.) was known for: His deep commitment to authentic Islamic teachings His emphasis on structured tarbiyat and discipline His dedication to women’s religious empowerment His ability to inspire unity, sincerity, and service His passing was deeply felt by scholars, students, and communities across regions. The large gatherings, heartfelt tributes, and collective grief reflected the immense respect and love he commanded. His departure created a profound void, yet his mission and vision continue to illuminate the path forward. Today, the continued growth and success of Jamia-tuz-Zahra stand as a living testimony to his sincerity, foresight, and lifelong sacrifice. May Allah elevate his ranks and grant him the highest rank in the hereafter.

"Educating a girl is like planting a tree whose shade benefits generations to come."
Co-Founder

Syeda Rabab Zaidi

Principal of Jamiatuz zahra

Syeda Rabab Zaidi is a distinguished Islamic scholar, educator, and reformer with over three decades of dedicated service in the field of religious education and women’s empowerment. Born in Patna, India, she comes from a respected scholarly lineage tracing back to the Ahlul Bayt (peace be upon them). She completed her advanced Hawza studies at Jamiatuz Zahra (Qom, Iran) and holds a Bachelor’s degree in Persian Literature. She was among the first Indian women to travel to Iran for formal religious studies, marking a historic milestone in women’s Islamic education. Since returning to India, she has played a pioneering role in establishing and leading institutions for women’s religious education. Under her guidance, structured Hawza programs, daily sections for university students, Qur’anic centers, and foundational maktab programs have flourished. After the demise of her husband, Alhajj Maulana Syed Hiader Mahdi Zaidi(T.S) in 2011, she assumed full responsibility for the academic and administrative leadership of the institution. She is widely respected for her lectures across India and internationally, her dedication to Tarbiyat, and her contributions to Islamic scholarship and community development. Her efforts have been recognized with multiple national and international awards, including the prestigious Goharshad International Award.

"Every student is a trust from Allah — we nurture them with knowledge, love, and Taqwa."

What We Teach

Courses We Offer

Jamia-tuz-Zahra offers a range of structured programs designed to provide authentic Islamic education along with moral and spiritual development. Each course is carefully planned according to age group, learning level, and commitment capacity, while maintaining our core focus on discipline and tarbiyat.

01

Jamiatuz Zahra – Hauzawi Course

6 Years Age: 14–18 Years Fully Residential

The Hauzawi Course is our flagship six-year comprehensive Islamic studies program designed for students who aspire to pursue higher religious education in a structured and disciplined residential environment. This intensive program provides in-depth study of Arabic Grammar (Nahw & Sarf), Fiqh (Islamic Jurisprudence), Usul, Aqaaid (Islamic Theology), Tafseer, and Akhlaq & Tarbiyat. The residential system ensures continuous supervision, structured daily routine, and practical implementation of Islamic values — shaping not only knowledgeable students but morally upright individuals.

Fiqh Tafseer Arabic Grammar Aqaaid Akhlaq
02

Madrasa-e-Ilmiyah Narjis

Daily Section of Jamiatuz Zahra

2 Years Age: 15–30 Years Day Scholars

This two-year program is designed for day scholars who wish to gain foundational Islamic knowledge without enrolling in the full residential system. The course focuses on Basic Fiqh and Aqaaid, Qur'anic studies, Islamic manners and practical rulings, and essential religious knowledge for daily life. It is ideal for students seeking structured Islamic education alongside their regular academic or personal commitments.

Basic Fiqh Aqaaid Quranic Studies
03

Short-Term 40 Days Islamic Camp

40 Days Age: 15–30 Years

This intensive short-term program is designed for individuals who wish to dedicate a focused period to spiritual growth and foundational Islamic learning. The camp includes daily Qur'an recitation, basic Islamic rulings, spiritual sessions, Akhlaq and self-development training, and structured worship routine. It serves as a transformative experience, strengthening faith, discipline, and religious understanding within a short period.

Self-Development Quran Recitation Akhlaq
04

Gahwara-e-Bibi Masooma

Maktab of Jamiatuz Zahra

5 Years Age: 3–9 Years Evening: 5–7 PM

Gahwara-e-Bibi Masooma is the foundational maktab program designed for young girls, providing early Islamic education in a caring and structured environment. The curriculum includes basic Qur'an learning, Duas and short surahs, Islamic manners and character building, basic beliefs and practical teachings, and moral storytelling and foundational tarbiyat. This program aims to build a strong Islamic foundation from early childhood, nurturing love for faith, discipline, and good character.

Surahs Akhlaq Tarbiyyah
05

Training Camp for Muballeghaat

40 Days For Educators

The 40-day Deeni Ta'leemi wa Tarbiyati Training Camp is specially designed for Muballeghaat and Maktab teachers who are actively engaged in religious education and community service. This program serves as a refresher and professional development course to strengthen both academic understanding and practical teaching skills. The camp includes revision of core Islamic sciences, teaching methodology and classroom management, effective communication and public speaking skills, spiritual development and moral refinement, and practical guidance for community leadership.

Teaching Methodology Public Speaking Community Leadership

New Admissions

Admissions Open 2025-26

Jamia-tuz-Zahra invites applications from dedicated and sincere students who wish to pursue authentic Islamic education in a spiritually nurturing and disciplined environment. Our institution follows a fully residential system, where hostel accommodation is compulsory for all students.

Our objective is not limited to academic instruction alone — we emphasize comprehensive Tarbiyat (moral, spiritual, and character development) alongside structured Islamic studies.

Our Vision: To prepare knowledgeable, disciplined, and morally upright individuals who can serve the faith and society with wisdom, dignity, and responsibility.

Eligibility: Prescribed age limit • Basic Qur'an reading ability • Commitment to discipline • Willingness to stay in hostel

Documents Required: Registration form • Academic mark sheets • ID proof • Passport photos

Admissions are granted strictly on merit, and seats remain limited to ensure quality education and focused tarbiyat.
